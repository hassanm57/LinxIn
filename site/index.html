What is LinxIn?

>_ /linxin --describe

LinxIn is an open-source AI job-search system that runs locally on your machine, inside any AI coding CLI. You paste a listing or scan company portals, and it evaluates each role on a three-part model, Fit, Reality and Trust, so you only pursue jobs that are a genuine match, that you can actually be hired for from where you live, and that are real rather than a scam. For anything worth pursuing it tailors your CV, drafts a cover letter, writes freelance proposals, and preps you for the interview. Everything stays on your machine: no cloud, no telemetry, no account. MIT-licensed and free forever; the only thing you pay for is the AI CLI you already use.

Three scores, not one.

Most tools ask “is this a good job?” LinxIn asks the three questions that actually decide it. A role has to clear all three.

Fit

Fit

Should you even want this? Skills, seniority, growth, and how the pay matches your goals.

Reality

Reality

Can a you-in-Pakistan actually get hired and live on it? Geo-eligibility, timezone, how you get paid, and what the salary is really worth in rupees.

Trust

Trust

Is it legit, or a scam or ghost job? Tuned for local fraud: upfront fees, WhatsApp recruiters, CNIC-before-offer.

Frequently asked

How does LinxIn score a job?
Every role gets three independent 0–100 scores. Fit (skills, seniority, comp, growth), Reality (geo-eligibility, timezone, payment path, pay in real purchasing power), and Trust (legitimacy and scam signals). You set thresholds; a job is worth pursuing only if it clears all three. Nothing is guessed, unknowns stay unknown.
Is it really free?
Yes. LinxIn is MIT-licensed, open source, and free forever. No paid tier, no account, no telemetry. The only cost is whichever AI CLI you already pay for.
Is my data private?
Completely. Everything runs on your machine and reads local plain-text files. Your CV, profile and pipeline never leave your computer, and the dashboard only serves to localhost.
Does it apply to jobs for me?
No. LinxIn is a co-pilot, not an autopilot. It evaluates and drafts; you decide and submit. There is no bulk auto-apply.
Why “built for Pakistan and remote-anywhere”?
Because hunting from the Global South has different hard problems: “remote” jobs that are secretly geo-locked, scams on local boards, and salaries that need translating into real purchasing power. LinxIn’s Reality and Trust scores are built around exactly those.
Who built it?
LinxIn was built by Hassan Mansoor, inspired by the local-first spirit of career-ops, and re-centered on the realities of job hunting from Pakistan and the wider Global South.
